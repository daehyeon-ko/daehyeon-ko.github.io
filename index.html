About

I am a mechanical engineering researcher with a background in advanced materials, micro/nanoscale fabrication, and laser-based manufacturing. My research focuses on developing functional material structures and electronic device platforms through controlled fabrication, materials processing, and device integration.


My recent work includes laser-induced electrode patterning, metal nanoparticle-based conductive materials, and soft electronic device integration. I am interested in exploring how advanced materials and micro/nanoscale fabrication strategies can be used to develop next-generation electronic, energy, and multifunctional device systems.

Research

Laser-Induced Photothermal Reduction for Microscale Electrode Patterning on Single Microfibers

Developed a laser-induced fabrication strategy for forming conductive silver pathways on individual nylon microfibers using a laser-reactive composite coating. This work focuses on localized materials processing, mask-free electrode patterning, and microscale device fabrication on curved and one-dimensional substrates.

Laser processing Micro/nanoscale fabrication Silver electrodes Functional materials

Fast Synthesis of Metal Nanoparticle Ink and Flexible Electrodes

Developed a rapid synthesis strategy for metal nanoparticle-based conductive ink and flexible electrodes. This work aims to support scalable materials processing and electronic device fabrication using solution-processable conductive materials.

Metal nanoparticle ink Conductive materials Materials processing Electronic devices
