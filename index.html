Profile

My research interests lie at the intersection of nanomaterials, micro/nanoscale fabrication, and optics-based materials processing. I focus on developing fabrication strategies that use laser processing, localized material transformation, and nanomaterial integration to create functional structures for electronic and energy-related device platforms.


My recent studies include high-precision laser patterning of conductive pathways on geometrically challenging microfiber substrates, synthesis of metal nanoparticles for conductive materials, and laser-assisted electrode fabrication. These experiences have shaped my broader interest in using advanced materials and optical/microfabrication techniques to engineer functional device architectures.

Laser-Induced Photothermal Reduction for Microscale Electrode Patterning on Single Microfibers

Developed a laser-based fabrication strategy for creating high-precision microscale silver electrodes on highly curved single nylon microfibers using a photo-reactive composite coating. This work focuses on overcoming the geometric challenges of electrode patterning on one-dimensional curved substrates through localized laser processing and mask-free conductive pathway formation.

Metal Nanoparticles Synthesis and Flexible Electrode Fabrication

Developed a facile synthesis approach for nickel- and copper-based nanoparticles and applied them to conductive inks for flexible electrode fabrication through laser processing. This work focuses on scalable nanomaterial synthesis, solution-processable conductive materials, and laser-based fabrication of nanomaterial-based electronic devices.
