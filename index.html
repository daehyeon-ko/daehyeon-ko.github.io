About

I am a mechanical engineering researcher with experience in advanced materials, soft electronic devices, and laser-based microfabrication. My research focuses on developing micro/nanoscale conductive structures on flexible and fiber-shaped platforms for next-generation wearable and soft electronic systems.


My current work explores laser-written silver electrodes on single microfibers, material design for soft electronic interfaces, and functional device integration using flexible conductive materials.

Research

Laser-Induced Photothermal Reduction for Microscale Electrode Patterning on Single Microfiber

Developed a laser-induced fabrication strategy for forming conductive silver pathways on individual nylon microfibers using a laser-reactive composite coating. This work aims to enable fiber-shaped electronic devices with localized, mask-free, and designable electrode patterns.
